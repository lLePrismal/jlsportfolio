Playing Guitar

Why I love playing guitar?

In the chaos of a weary day, it becomes my calm.

Each strum is a whisper of comfort, each note a balm to my restless mind.

The strings don’t just play music—they untangle my thoughts, reset my spirit, and turn exhaustion into harmony.

With every chord, the weight of the day fades, and what remains is freedom, fire, and soul.

Painting

Painting has always been my childhood refuge. (check my latest paintings here)

While others found comfort in play, mine was found in colors.

Every scolding, every tear, every small wound of growing up— I carried them to the shore, where sand and silence listened.

There, with my hands and scraps of color, I painted my pain away.

Brushstrokes became my solace, sketches my escape, and art my secret language of healing.

Today, that same comfort lives on in my canvases— each piece a memory, a confession, a fragment of who I am.

Fishing

These days, my soul aches for the sea.

For the scent of salt carried by the wind, for the invisible touch of negative ions that reset my weary body clock.

I crave the waves that whisper of forgotten childhood mornings, when time was slower, gentler, and the ocean held all the answers.

And so, I turn to fishing— not merely to catch, but to remember.

Each cast of the line is a reaching back, each ripple on the water a conversation with the past.

In the stillness between tides, I find what I’ve been longing for: peace, memory, and the rhythm of life restored.

And so, these three loves—my guitar, my brush, and the sea— bring color, fragrance, and comfort back into a life sometimes dulled by the weight of days.

Perhaps next time, you could join me by the shore— to breathe the ocean air, to laugh, to dream, and let the horizon wash our worries away.

Ah, and how could I forget—the laughter shared with friends, a bucket of cold beer at our side, and the kind of company that makes every moment taste a little sweeter.
